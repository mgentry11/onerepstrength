The 30-20-40 Protocol

One rep. 90 seconds. Maximum results. The protocol focuses on time under tension rather than counting reps. Research shows 60-90 seconds of continuous muscle tension stimulates optimal muscle growth.

Lower for 30

Begin with a controlled 30-second eccentric phase. Your muscles can handle 40% more load when lowering.

Lift for 20

Raise the weight slowly for 20 seconds. No momentum, no jerking. Pure muscle contraction.

Lower for 40

The final 40-second negative is where the magic happens. Deep muscle fatigue triggers growth.

Why It Works

Negative-accentuated training emphasizes the eccentric (lowering) phase of exercises. Your muscles can handle approximately 40% more load when lowering than when lifting, and research shows eccentric training produces up to 50% greater strength gains.

The 30-20-40 protocol dedicates 70 of its 90 seconds to negative phases. This isn't arbitrary - it's based on decades of research from exercise scientists and practical application by trainers like Dr. Ellington Darden.

For adults over 50, this approach is ideal: maximum muscle stimulation with minimal joint stress. No heavy weights bouncing around. No momentum. Just controlled, focused movement that builds real strength safely.

The Workouts

Two alternating workouts, each targeting all major muscle groups. Train 2-3 times per week, alternating between Workout A and Workout B.

Workout A

Leg Press, Leg Curl, Leg Extension, Chest Press, Seated Row, Shoulder Press, Lat Pulldown, Bicep Curl

Workout B

Leg Press, Leg Curl, Calf Raise, Chest Fly, Lat Pulldown, Lateral Raise, Tricep Extension, Ab Crunch
